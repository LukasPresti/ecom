Section 3

Usage & Listing Policy

3.1 Listing Standards

All Product Listings on CampusThrift must adhere to the following standards:

  • Accuracy: Item descriptions must be accurate and comprehensive. Listings must include, to the best of the lister's knowledge, the condition and functionality of the item, and additionally include any known defects or features that a reasonable Buyer would want to know.
  • Photography: All listings must have at least two up-to-date photos of the actual item being sold. Diagrams, outdated photos, or stock photos must be clearly marked, and may not be used as primary listing photos.
  • Comprehensiveness: Listings must include all required information at reasonable detail, including the item's category, condition, campus location, purchase status, tags, and any other reasonable information.
  • Good Faith Listing: Items must be real, and available at the price, location, and time listed. Listing items you do not intend to sell, misrepresenting the price you are seeking for them or where they can be delivered, or making bad faith offers are prohibited.

3.2 Messaging Standards

CampusThrift's messaging system is provided solely to facilitate the transactions between users of the Platform. Acceptable use of this service includes:

  • Asking questions about an item listed
  • Good-faith negotiations over price
  • Coordinating logistics, such as pickup times or locations

The following uses of the messaging system are prohibited:

  • Spam or unprompted promotional content of any kind
  • Requesting or communicating any manner of personal information beyond that strictly necessary for the proper usage of the app, including financial information or personal history
  • Harassment, defamatory language, threats, hate speech, or other offensive speech
  • Sharing contact information with the intent to bypass CampusThrift's platform or payment system
  • Any manner of bad-faith negotiation or communication, such as leading on buyers or sellers, making unreasonable price negotiations, or badgering other users
  • Any communication related to or facilitating activities prohibited by local, state, or federal law, or in violation of CampusThrift's Terms and Policies

3.3 Prohibited Conduct

You agree you will not use the platform to:

  • Make false, misleading, or deceptive listings
  • Make any listing in violation of the above described Listing Standards
  • Use CampusThrift's messaging system in a way inconsistent with the above Messaging Standards
  • Engage in any form of fraud or criminal behavior
  • Create multiple accounts
  • Attempt to dox or collect information from other users without their consent
  • Engage in any form of harassment, bullying, or hate speech
  • Use automated tools, bots, or scrapers to interact with the Platform in any manner
  • Reproduce CampusThrift listings, content, or intellectual property on outside platforms
  • Attempt to circumvent CampusThrift policy by continuing business or communication off the Platform after initial contact was made through CampusThrift

Section 5

Return & Refund Policy

5.1 Overarching Policy

CampusThrift is a peer to peer marketplace, so all sales are final unless otherwise stated in the listing or required by law in extenuating circumstances. With this being said, buyers must carefully review item details, photos, and communication with the seller before purchasing.

5.2 Return Eligibility

Returns may only be allowed if:

  • The item is materially different from the listing — wrong item, undisclosed significant or noticeable damage, or missing components.
  • The item was not suitable to wear as described (clothing only).
  • The item was not delivered as stated.

Returns are not allowed for:

  • Minor cosmetic issues depicted in photos available on the listing.
  • Buyer's remorse.
  • Damage caused after the item is delivered.

5.3 Return Request Process

Buyers must submit a return request within 48 hours of receiving the item or after the stated time of a failed delivery/pickup, alongside photo and communication evidence. Requests made after the 48 hour window may be denied.

5.4 Seller Responsibilities

If a return is approved by CampusThrift, the seller party will accept the return and coordinate the best return method with priority being to the buyer for their preferred return method. Refunds must be issued after the item is returned in its original condition. CampusThrift will step in and review disputes if the seller disagrees.

5.5 Refund Process

Refunds are given through either the original payment method or the preferred method of the buyer, but may take several business days. Partial refunds will be granted if only part of the listing was misrepresented or insignificant or slightly less than noticeable damage occurs to the item before delivery.

5.6 Non-Delivery and Cancellations

If a Seller fails to deliver as agreed, the Buyer may request a full refund. Cancellation requests made before delivery are not guaranteed and may depend on Seller circumstances.

5.7 Abuse of Policy and CampusThrift Involvement in Disputes

CampusThrift may deny requests involving false claims or misuse of the system. Repeated abuse may result in account suspension or termination.

CampusThrift may assist in dispute resolution but does not guarantee refunds. Final decisions are made at its discretion based on available evidence.

Section 6

Community Guidelines

6.1 Respectful Community

As a Peer to Peer commerce network for university students, professional and respectful interactions are the cornerstone of the service. We implore users to respect each other and enter good faith agreements.

  • Zero tolerance for harassment as outlined in Section 3.2 Messaging Standards.
  • Respectful and good faith communication is required.

6.2 Harboring Trust

CampusThrift relies on the community, and the community needs to trust each other.

  • Honest Listings: Accurately represent the condition, brand, and functionality of your items. Do not use stock photos or attempt to hide flaws (Section 3.1).
  • No Flaking or Ghosting: If you agree to a price and a meetup time/location, honor your commitment.
  • Fair Negotiation: Haggling is part of thrifting, but lowballing to an extreme degree, leading buyers/sellers on, or making bad-faith offers is prohibited.
  • No Commercial Selling: CampusThrift is for personal, used items only. Businesses, commercial resellers, and drop-shippers are strictly prohibited from using the platform (Section 1.4).

6.3 Creating a Safe Platform

Because CampusThrift often involves in-person item handoffs, your physical and digital safety is our top priority.

  • Safe Meetups: We strongly encourage users to meet in well-lit, public locations on campus during daylight hours.
  • Protect Your Data: Keep all communication regarding transactions within the CampusThrift messaging system.
  • Keep Payments on the Platform: Attempting to bypass CampusThrift's payment system puts you at risk of fraud. To ensure you are protected by our platform, all financial transactions must be completed through our approved third-party payment processors (Section 4).

6.4 Accountability and Reporting

We rely on our community to help keep CampusThrift safe and functional.

  • Report Bad Behavior: If you encounter a user who is violating these guidelines, engaging in fraud, or making you feel unsafe, please use the in-app reporting tools immediately.
  • Enforcement: CampusThrift reserves the right to remove listings, issue warnings, or permanently terminate the accounts of users who violate these Community Guidelines, our Terms of Service, or our Usage Policies (Section 1.5).
